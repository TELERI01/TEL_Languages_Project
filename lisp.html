LISP

LISP (LISt Processing) is a family of programming languages known for their unique syntax and powerful features for symbolic computing.

Overview

LISP was developed in the late 1950s by John McCarthy at MIT. It has undergone various dialects and implementations over the years.

Usage

LISP is used in artificial intelligence research, computer algebra systems, and other domains requiring symbolic computation and manipulation of symbolic expressions.
